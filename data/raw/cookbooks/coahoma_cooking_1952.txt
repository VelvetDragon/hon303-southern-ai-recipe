**CREOLE CASSEROLE**

**Ingredients:**

- 1 cup grated Cheddar cheese

- 1 recipe Creole sauce
- 2 cups thick white sauce
- 3 tablespoons grated cheese

**ICE BOX ROLLS**

**Ingredients:**
- 1 quart sweet milk
- 1 cup shortening
- 1 scant cup sugar
- 2 teaspoons salt
- 1 teaspoon baking soda
- 1 tablespoon baking powder
- 2 yeast cakes
- 1 quart flour (plus additional to make stiff dough)
- 1 teaspoon sugar (for yeast)

**Steps:**
1. Scald milk, then add melted shortening and sugar. Cool to lukewarm.
2. Dissolve yeast cakes in warm water with 1 teaspoon sugar, then add to the cooled milk mixture.
3. Let the mixture stand for 2 hours until doubled in size (use a large pan).
4. Sift flour with salt, baking soda, and baking powder.
5. Add the flour mixture to the yeast mixture and beat with an electric beater until a light batter forms.
6. Add enough additional flour to make a stiff dough.
7. Store in the refrigerator and use as needed.

**LEMON TARTS**

**Ingredients:**
- 1/2 cup flour
- 1 cup sugar
- 2 cups boiling water
- 2 tablespoons butter
- 4 egg yolks
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 3 lemons (grated rinds and juice, add more if needed)
- 4 egg whites (for meringue)
- Pinch of salt (for meringue)

**Steps:**

8. Top the tarts with meringue and bake until golden.

**EGG PLANT AND STUFFED SQUASH**

**Ingredients:**
- 1 eggplant (2 lbs)
- 1 can tomatoes
- 2 cups chopped onions
- 1 small can tomato paste (if needed)
- 1 can tomato soup
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 2 tablespoons bacon drippings
- 1/2 cup chopped green peppers
- 2 cups chopped celery
- Worcestershire sauce, to taste
- Salt, to taste
- Pepper, to taste
- Grated cheese, as needed
- Broiled buttered shrimp, for garnish
- Chopped parsley, for garnish
- 4 medium yellow squash
- 1/2 cup water
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1/8 teaspoon black pepper
- 2 tablespoons butter
- 1 finely chopped onion
- 1/2 cup cooked chopped celery
- Grated chicken liver (optional)
- 2 tablespoons cream
- Paprika, for garnish

**Steps:**
1. **Eggplant:**
   - Wash and dry eggplant, cut off a thin lengthwise slice, and scoop out the inside, leaving a 1/4 to 1/2-inch thick wall.
   - Sprinkle salt inside the shell and set in water.
   - Cut eggplant flesh into pieces, cover with water, add a little salt, and cook until tender. Drain.
   - Cook onions, celery, and green peppers in bacon drippings until they begin to brown.
   - Add tomatoes and cook for 10 minutes, then add tomato soup (and tomato paste if needed). Cook slowly until thick.
   - Add chopped eggplant and season highly to taste with Worcestershire, salt, and pepper.
   - Fill eggplant shells with alternating layers of eggplant mixture and grated cheese, topping with cheese.
   - Bake until the cheese is golden brown.
   - Garnish with broiled buttered shrimp dipped in chopped parsley, placed in each scallop around the eggplant.
2. **Squash:**
   - Halve squash lengthwise, careful not to break the neck, and scoop out seeds.
   - Place shells in water with 1/2 cup water and 1 teaspoon salt, and cook until tender.
   - Drain squash and mash the flesh.
   - In a skillet, melt butter, add finely chopped onion, and cook until it begins to brown.
   - Add mashed squash, cooked celery, black pepper, and 2 tablespoons cream. Season to taste.
   - Fill squash shells with the mixture, sprinkle with grated cheese and paprika.
   - Bake until heated through. Serves 6 to 8.

**RUM PUDDING**

**Ingredients:**
- 3 egg whites
- 1 cup whipping cream
- 2 tablespoons Knox gelatin
- 3 egg yolks
- 1/2 cup sugar
- 1/4 cup rum

**Steps:**
1. Soak gelatin in 1/2 cup cool water in the top of a double boiler, then dissolve over hot water.
2. Beat egg yolks until very light, then slowly beat in sugar using an electric beater.
3. Slowly add melted hot gelatin to the egg yolk mixture, then add rum drop by drop while beating.
4. Set aside to cool and begin setting.
5. Beat egg whites until very stiff and dry.
6. When the rum mixture begins to congeal, fold in the egg whites and whipped cream.
7. Pour into a mold and refrigerate until set.

- 3/4 cup butter
- 3/4 cup milk
- 3/4 teaspoon cloves
- 1 3/4 cups sugar
- 2 tablespoons cocoa
- 1 teaspoon vanilla
- 1 1/2 teaspoons cinnamon
- 2 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
- 4 eggs
- 2 cups cake flour (sifted before measuring)
- Caramel filling (recommended)

**Steps:**
1. Cream butter, add sugar, and beat until light and fluffy.
2. Add whole eggs, beating well after each addition.
3. Sift flour, baking powder, cocoa, cinnamon, and cloves together.
4. Add vanilla to milk.
5. Alternately add the flour mixture and milk to the butter mixture, beating until smooth.
6. Pour into two greased and floured 9-inch layer cake pans.
7. Bake at 375°F for 25–35 minutes.
8. Use caramel filling or your favorite filling.

**BRULA ICE CREAM**

**Ingredients:**

- 3/4 cup sugar

- 1 pint cream

**Steps:**

**TOMATO ASPIC - PRESSED CHICKEN RING MOLD**

**Ingredients:**
- 2 tablespoons gelatin
- 1/2 cup cold water
- 2 small No. 2 cans tomato juice
- Garlic salt or garlic vinegar, to taste
- Lemon juice, to taste
- Onion salt, to taste
- Celery salt, to taste
- Tabasco sauce, to taste
- Worcestershire sauce, to taste
- Brown sugar, to taste
- Paprika, to taste
- 2 cups cubed cooked chicken
- 1 cup sliced hard-cooked eggs
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice
- 3/4 cup sliced olives
- 2 tablespoons unflavored gelatin
- 1 cup chopped celery
- Avocado dressing, for serving
- Lettuce, for serving

**Steps:**
1. Soak gelatin in cold water, then dissolve in heated (not boiled) tomato juice.
2. Season with garlic salt, lemon juice, onion salt, celery salt, Tabasco, Worcestershire, brown sugar, and paprika to taste.
3. Pour into a ring mold rinsed with cold water or greased with Wesson oil.
4. Refrigerate until firm.
5. For the pressed chicken, dissolve 2 tablespoons gelatin in 1/2 cup cold water, then add to 2 cups boiling chicken cooking liquid.
6. Season and chill until syrupy.
7. Mix with cubed chicken, sliced eggs, lemon juice, olives, and celery.
8. When the mixture begins to set, pour over the set tomato aspic in the ring mold.
9. Refrigerate until fully set.
10. Serve on lettuce with avocado dressing.

**GLAZED BAKED HAM**

- Whole cloves (optional)

- Water, for glaze

**MRS. E. M. FANT’S AMERICAN PLUM PUDDING**

- 1 cup peach preserves
- 1 cup buttermilk

- 1 pound raisins
