**CHEESE PUFFS**

**Ingredients:**
- 1/2 c. margarine
- 1 c. sifted flour
- 2 c. shredded cheese
- 1/2 tsp. paprika
- 1/4 tsp. red pepper
- 1/2 tsp. salt

**Steps:**
1. Cream margarine and add cheese; cream well.
2. Add seasoning to flour and combine with cheese mixture; blend well.
3. Shape into 1-inch balls and freeze or chill thoroughly.
4. Place on cookie sheet and bake at 350°F for 15 minutes.
5. Serve with salads.

**GARLIC CHEESE STRAWS**

**Ingredients:**
- 1/2 c. shortening
- 1 c. shredded sharp American cheese
- 3 Tbsp. grated Parmesan cheese
- 3/4 tsp. garlic salt
- 1 c. sifted regular flour

**Steps:**
1. Cream shortening and add cheese; beat well.

**SWEET-DILL PICKLES**

**Ingredients:**
- 2 gal. cucumbers
- Alum powder (1 Tbsp. per gal. of water)
- Dill sprigs
- 6 lb. sugar
- 2 pt. white sugar
- 1 box pickling spice
- 1 1/2 Tbsp. tarragon vinegar

**Steps:**
1. Wash cucumbers and soak in brine (strong enough to float an egg, non-iodized salt) for 8 days.
2. Soak in clear water for 24 hours.
3. Cut into strips and soak in alum water (1 Tbsp. alum per gal. water) for 48 hours.
4. Rinse cucumbers and jars.
5. Place cucumbers and dill sprigs in jars, cover with hot syrup made from sugar and pickling spice.
6. Add 1 1/2 Tbsp. tarragon vinegar the next morning.
7. Weigh down and let set for 7-10 days.
8. Pack in quart jars with a dill sprig, heat syrup, and seal.

**CRISP SWEET CUCUMBER PICKLES**

**Ingredients:**
- 7 lb. cucumbers, sliced crosswise
- 2 c. pickling lime
- 2 gal. water
- 2 qt. vinegar
- 4 1/2 lb. sugar
- 1 Tbsp. salt
- 1/2 can (1 1/2 oz.) pickling spices
- 6-8 cinnamon sticks

**Steps:**
1. Soak cucumbers in 2 c. pickling lime mixed with 2 gal. water for 24 hours.
2. Rinse in 3 different cool waters, then soak in ice water for 3 hours and remove.
3. Make syrup with vinegar, sugar, salt, pickling spices (in a cloth bag), and cinnamon sticks.
4. Pour syrup over cucumbers and let set overnight.
5. Boil mixture for 35 minutes or until cucumber slices are clear.
6. Can in jars, adding cinnamon sticks if desired.

- 1 large can mushroom pieces
- 3 medium onions, sliced
- 1 head Chinese cabbage, shredded

- 1 tsp. accent
- 1 tsp. salt
- 1 can bean sprouts
- 1 can sukiyaki (no tomo) or 1 can bamboo shoots and 1 additional can mushroom pieces

- Cooking oil

1. In a buttered casserole dish, layer white asparagus, cream of celery soup, and sliced water chestnuts alternately.
2. Sprinkle cracker crumbs over the top and dot with butter or margarine.
3. Bake at 350°F until bubbling hot, about 25-30 minutes.
