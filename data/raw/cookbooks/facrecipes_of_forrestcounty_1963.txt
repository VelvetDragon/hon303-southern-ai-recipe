**BEEF STROGANOFF**

**Ingredients:**
- 7 tablespoons flour
- 3 tablespoons shortening
- 2 pounds beef for stew, cut in ½-inch cubes
- 1½ teaspoons salt
- ¼ teaspoon pepper
- 1 cup chopped onion
- 1 cup tomato juice
- 2 cups liquid (water and liquid from mushrooms)
- 2 bouillon cubes
- 1 can (4 oz) mushrooms
- 1 cup sour cream

**Steps:**
1. Rub beef with garlic.
2. Combine 6 tablespoons flour, salt, and pepper, reserving 1 tablespoon flour for later; dredge meat in seasoned flour.
3. Brown meat in shortening in a skillet, then remove.
4. Sauté onion in skillet drippings, drain excess grease, and return meat to skillet.
5. Dissolve bouillon cubes in 1½ cups liquid, add to meat with tomato juice, cover, and simmer for 1½ hours.
6. Add mushrooms.
7. Combine reserved 1 tablespoon flour with remaining ½ cup liquid, add to skillet, and stir until mixture thickens.
8. Heat thoroughly and fold in sour cream just before serving.
9. Serve over cooked noodles or parsleyed rice.

**COUNTRY CAPTAIN**

- 1 onion, chopped

- ¼ cup raisins
- ¼ cup almonds
- 1 teaspoon curry powder
- 1 teaspoon thyme
- Salt, to taste
- Pepper, to taste
- 3 tablespoons corn oil
- 1 can (4 oz) mushrooms

**SHRIMP STUFFED EGGPLANT**

**Ingredients:**
- 1 stalk celery, diced
- 1 quart soft bread crumbs
- 1 large eggplant
- 3 tablespoons butter or olive oil
- 1½ pounds shrimp, fresh or frozen
- 1 medium onion, chopped
- 1/3 cup water
- ¾ teaspoon oregano
- ½ cup fine dried bread crumbs
- 1 tablespoon melted butter
- Parsley, for garnish

**Steps:**
1. Shell and devein shrimp.
2. Cut eggplant lengthwise into halves, scrape out pulp leaving ½-inch thick shells, and cook pulp in water just to cover until tender (about 15 minutes); drain well.
3. Cook shrimp in boiling salted water for 3 minutes, drain, soak in cold water, and dice, reserving a few whole shrimp for garnish.
4. Melt 3 tablespoons butter in a skillet, add onion and celery, and cook until tender but not browned.
5. Add soft bread crumbs, water, salt, oregano, cooked eggplant, and diced shrimp to skillet; toss gently until well mixed.
6. Fill eggplant shells with mixture.
7. Combine dried bread crumbs with 1 tablespoon melted butter and sprinkle over shrimp mixture.
8. Bake at 350°F for 30 minutes or until crumbs are browned.
9. Garnish with whole shrimp and parsley.

**COLOSSAL CORN BREAD**

**Ingredients:**
- 1 package active dry yeast or cake yeast
- ¼ cup very warm water
- 2 cups milk, scalded
- 1/3 cup granulated sugar
- 1/3 cup shortening
- 1 tablespoon salt
- 3 cups sifted all-purpose flour
- 2 eggs, well-beaten
- 1 cup yellow corn meal
- 4 to 4½ cups sifted all-purpose flour

**Steps:**
1. Sprinkle yeast into very warm water in a measuring cup and stir until dissolved.
2. In a large mixing bowl, combine scalded milk, sugar, shortening, and salt; stir in 3 cups flour until blended.
3. Add eggs, yeast mixture, and corn meal; gradually stir in 4 cups flour.
4. On a lightly floured board, knead dough for 10 minutes until smooth and plastic, using remaining flour as needed.
5. Place dough in a large greased bowl, turning to grease all sides; cover with a towel and let rise in a warm place (85°F) for about 1½ hours or until doubled in size.
6. Grease two Pyrex loaf pans.
7. Turn dough onto a floured surface, knead for 1–2 minutes, shape into loaves, and place seam side down in pans.
8. Let rise in a warm place until doubled in size.
9. Bake at 375°F (or 350°F for Pyrex) for 35–45 minutes.
10. Turn out to cool on their sides on a wire rack.

**REFRIGERATOR ROLLS**

**Ingredients:**
- 1¾ cups warm water (110–115°F)
- 2 packages active dry yeast
- ½ cup granulated sugar
- 1 tablespoon salt
- 1 egg, unbeaten
- ¼ cup soft butter or margarine
- 6 cups sifted all-purpose flour
- 1 tablespoon butter, melted

**Steps:**
1. Pour warm water into a large bowl (rinsed with hot water) and check temperature to ensure it’s warm, not hot.
2. Sprinkle yeast over water, add sugar and salt, and stir to dissolve completely.
3. Add egg, soft butter, and 3 cups flour; beat with a wooden spoon or electric mixer at medium speed for 2 minutes until smooth.
4. Gradually add 1 cup flour, beating hard after each addition, then work in remaining 2 cups flour with hands until smooth and elastic.
5. Brush the top of the dough with melted butter, cover with a double thickness of plastic wrap or a damp towel, and let rise in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours or until doubled in bulk.
6. Punch down dough and refrigerate, punching down once a day for 1–3 days.
7. Shape into rolls, place in greased pans, and let rise in a warm place until doubled in size.
8. Bake at 400–425°F for 15–20 minutes, depending on roll shape and size.

**FIG CAKE**

**Ingredients:**
- 1½ cups butter
- 1 cup sugar
- 2 eggs
- 2 cups sifted flour
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1 teaspoon baking powder
- ½ teaspoon baking soda
- ½ teaspoon cinnamon
- ½ teaspoon cloves
- ½ cup buttermilk
- 1 cup fig juice
- 1 teaspoon vanilla
- 1 quart canned figs or 2 (1-pound 1 oz) jars, drained and chopped
- 1 cup chopped pecans
- ½ teaspoon cornstarch
- 1 cup brown sugar, packed
- ¼ cup milk
- 1¾ cups sifted confectioners’ sugar

**Steps:**
1. Cream 1 cup butter and sugar, then beat in eggs.
2. Sift flour, salt, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, and cloves together.
3. Combine buttermilk and 2/3 cup fig juice; add alternately with dry ingredients to the creamed mixture, beating until smooth.
4. Add vanilla, then fold in figs (reserving 2/3 cup for filling) and pecans.
5. Pour into two greased 8-inch round pans and bake at 325°F for over 1 hour.
6. For the filling, cook cornstarch with 1/3 cup fig juice until clear, then fold in reserved 2/3 cup figs; spread between layers.
7. For the frosting, melt ½ cup butter, add brown sugar, and boil over low heat for 2 minutes, stirring.
8. Add ¼ cup milk, bring to a boil, and cool.
9. Add confectioners’ sugar, beat until smooth, and frost the cake.

**FUDGE NUGGETS**

**Ingredients:**
- ¾ cup shortening
- 1 cup sugar
- 1 egg
- 1 teaspoon vanilla
- 2 (1 oz) squares unsweetened chocolate, melted and slightly cooled
- 2 cups sifted all-purpose flour
- ½ teaspoon salt
- 2 teaspoons instant coffee (dry)
- ½ cup milk
- 1 cup quick-cooking rolled oats
- ½ cup chopped nuts

**Steps:**
1. Cream shortening and sugar until fluffy, then beat in egg and vanilla.
2. Stir in melted chocolate.
3. Sift flour, salt, and instant coffee together; add to chocolate mixture alternately with milk.
4. Stir in rolled oats and nuts.
5. Drop by teaspoonfuls onto an ungreased cookie sheet.
6. Bake at 350°F for 15 minutes or until done.
7. Dust with confectioners’ sugar.

**LEMON-COCONUT COOKIES**

**Ingredients:**
- 1 cup butter
- 1½ cups sugar
- 1 egg
- ½ teaspoon grated lemon rind
- 2 cups sifted flour
- ¼ teaspoon salt
- 1 cup flaked coconut

**Steps:**
1. Cream butter, add sugar, and continue creaming until light and fluffy.
2. Add egg and grated lemon rind; beat well.
3. Sift flour and salt together, then stir into the creamed mixture.
4. Blend in flaked coconut.
5. Drop by teaspoonfuls onto an ungreased baking sheet.
6. Bake at 325°F for 15–20 minutes until lightly browned around the edges.
7. Remove from baking sheets and cool on wire racks.

**LITTLE PECAN PIES**

**Ingredients:**
- 1 (3 oz) package cream cheese
- 1 stick margarine
- 1 cup flour
- 1 egg
- 1 cup light brown sugar
- Dash of salt
- 1 tablespoon vanilla
- 2/3 cup chopped nuts

**Steps:**
1. Let cream cheese and margarine soften at room temperature, then cream together and gradually add flour.
2. Chill dough in the refrigerator for 2 hours.
3. Take small portions (about 1 teaspoon) of dough and press into small ungreased muffin tins to form shells.
4. For the filling, lightly beat egg, then add brown sugar, salt, vanilla, and nuts.
5. Spoon filling into pie shells, filling about 2/3 full.
6. Bake at 325°F for 12–15 minutes.
7. Cool in tins for about 10 minutes before removing.

**MOLASSES NUT CAKE**

**Ingredients:**
- ¾ cup butter or margarine
- ½ cup brown sugar, packed
- ¾ cup molasses
- 3 eggs, well-beaten
- 2½ cups sifted flour
- ¼ teaspoon salt
- ½ teaspoon baking powder
- ½ teaspoon baking soda
- ½ cup chopped nuts

**Steps:**
1. Cream butter and brown sugar, then blend in molasses.
2. Add eggs.
3. Sift flour, salt, baking powder, and baking soda together; blend nuts into dry ingredients.
4. Add dry ingredients gradually to the molasses mixture.
5. Pour into a greased 9 x 9 x 2-inch pan.
6. Bake at 350°F for 45 minutes.
7. Cool for 10 minutes before removing from pan.
8. For the frosting, melt a 6 oz package of chocolate pieces over hot water, cool, then blend with ½ cup dairy sour cream and 1 teaspoon vanilla; spread on cake.

**PINEAPPLE-CHEESE ICE BOX PIE**

**Ingredients:**
- 1 cup graham cracker crumbs
- 4 tablespoons melted margarine or butter
- 2 tablespoons sugar
- 1 tablespoon plain gelatin
- ¼ cup cold water
- 3 egg yolks, slightly beaten
- 1 cup crushed pineapple (with syrup)
- 1 teaspoon grated lemon peel
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice
- ¼ cup sugar
- ½ cup sugar
- 1 cup cottage cheese

**Steps:**
1. Mix graham cracker crumbs, melted margarine, and 2 tablespoons sugar; press into a 9-inch pie pan, reserving 3 tablespoons crumbs for topping.
2. Chill crust thoroughly or bake at 375°F for 8 minutes; cool.
3. Soften gelatin in cold water and set aside.
4. In a cold double boiler, combine egg yolks, pineapple, lemon peel, lemon juice, and ¼ cup sugar; cook over hot water, stirring until thick.
5. Add gelatin to the hot mixture, stirring until melted; remove from heat.
6. Put cottage cheese through a wire strainer and add to the hot mixture.
7. Heap filling into the chilled crust, sprinkle with reserved crumbs, and chill for 3 hours.

**PLAIN POUND CAKE**

**Ingredients:**
- 1 cup butter
- 1 2/3 cups sugar
- 5 eggs
- 2 cups flour
- ½ teaspoon baking powder

- ½ teaspoon vanilla
- 5 tablespoons milk

**Steps:**

**POWDERED SUGAR COOKIES**

**Ingredients:**

- ½ cup powdered sugar
- 2¼ cups flour
- Dash of salt
- 2 teaspoons vanilla
- ¾ cup chopped nut meats

**Steps:**
1. Cream butter and powdered sugar.

**VANILLA WAFER CAKE**

**Ingredients:**
- 2 sticks oleo
- 2 cups sugar
- 6 eggs
- 1 box (12 oz) vanilla wafers
- ½ cup milk
- 1 cup pecans, chopped
- 1 can (7 oz) angel flake coconut

**Steps:**
1. Cream oleo and sugar.
2. Crush vanilla wafers until very fine.
3. Add wafers to creamed mixture alternately with milk and eggs; blend well.
4. Add pecans and coconut.
5. Pour into a tube pan and bake at 325°F for 1½ hours or until firm and golden brown.
6. Let cool in the pan before removing.

**WAIOLI DATE BARS**

**Ingredients:**
- 2 cups dates
- ½ cup brown sugar
- ½ cup water
- 1 tablespoon flour
- 1 teaspoon vanilla
- 1¾ cups flour
- 1 teaspoon baking soda
- 2¼ cups quick rolled oats
- 1 cup brown sugar
- 1 cup butter, melted

**Steps:**
1. Cook dates, ½ cup brown sugar, water, and 1 tablespoon flour slowly until thick but soft; add vanilla and set aside.
2. Mix 1¾ cups flour, baking soda, oats, and 1 cup brown sugar; add melted butter to form a dry mixture.
3. Press half the dry mixture into a greased 8 x 8-inch pan.
4. Pour the date mixture over the dry layer.
5. Press the remaining dry mixture over the date layer.
6. Bake at 350°F for 20 minutes.
