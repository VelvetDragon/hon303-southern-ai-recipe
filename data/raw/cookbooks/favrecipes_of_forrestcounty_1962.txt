**ARRDP CON POLLO**

**Ingredients:**
- Stewing chicken, cut in pieces (Save 4 cups broth)
- 1½ pounds small shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 2 cans Mushrooms, stems and pieces
- 1 box frozen English Peas
- 1 can (4 oz.) Pimento-sliced
- 2 small onions, chopped fine
- ½ green pepper, chopped fine
- 2 cups canned tomatoes
- 2 teaspoons salt
- 2 teaspoons paprika
- 4 tablespoons fat
- 2 cups raw rice
- ½ cup parsley, chopped

**Steps:**
1. Boil chicken, save broth.
2. Brown rice in hot fat.
3. Add chicken broth, bring to boil and simmer.
4. When rice is half done add frozen peas, cubed chicken pieces, whole shrimp, mushrooms, pimento, onion, green pepper, salt, paprika and tomatoes.
5. Simmer until peas, shrimp and rice are done.
6. Stir with a fork occasionally.
7. Broth should be all absorbed.
8. Garnish with parsley.
9. Serve with Guacamole on lettuce cup garnished with tomato wedges in Mexican atmosphere.

**BEEF CASSEROLE**

**Ingredients:**
- 2 cups leftover pot roast or 1 pound ground beef
- 1 large onion
- 3 stalks celery
- 1 green pepper
- 2 tablespoons butter
- 1 can (10½ ounces) mushroom soup
- ½ cup water
- ½ teaspoon salt
- Dark pepper
- 1 can (3 ounce) chow mein noodles

**Steps:**
1. Start oven at 325°.
2. Cut onion, celery and pepper coarsely.
3. Chop left over beef in small cubes.
4. Cook beef in 1 tablespoon fat (butter) for several minutes (if ground beef, cook until brown) and transfer to medium size casserole.
5. Put remaining butter or margarine in pan, toss in chopped vegetables and cook until limp.
6. Stir in mushroom soup, water, salt, pepper and pour over beef, Mix well.
7. Cover and bake 30 minutes.
8. Remove cover and sprinkle with chow mein noodles and bake 10 minutes.

**CHICKEN LIVERS ON TOAST POINTS**

**Ingredients:**
- 2 pounds chicken livers
- 1 ¾ cups flour
- ⅛ teaspoon pepper
- 8 slices salt pork or country bacon
- 2 medium onions, finely chopped
- ½ pound thin sliced mushrooms
- 2 tablespoons butter
- 5 cups chicken broth
- 2 or 3 drops of Kitchen Bouquet, if needed
- Pinch nutmeg
- ½ cup sherry
- Toast Points
- Paprika
- 1 cup sour cream

**Steps:**
1. Cut livers in half.
2. Toss in 1 cup of flour mixed with ⅛ teaspoon pepper.
3. Saute pork or bacon until very crisp.
4. Remove, Drain on paper towels and keep warm.
5. Saute livers in drippings until cooked.
6. Remove, Drain on paper towels.
7. Saute onions until almost tender.
8. Add mushrooms and cook until tender.
9. Remove and add to the livers.
10. Add 2 tablespoons butter to the drippings and heat.
11. Stir in ¾ cup flour, Cook, stirring constantly until flour begins to brown.
12. Take care it doesn't burn.
13. Add chicken broth and stir until smooth and thickened.
14. If sauce looks anemic, add gravy coloring.
15. Season with nutmeg, salt and pepper to taste.
16. Add livers, onions and mushrooms to sauce.
17. Heat.
18. Stir in sour cream and sherry.
19. Serve on toast points.
20. Sprinkle crumbed pork or bacon over top and dust with paprika.
21. May be prepared ahead of time, reheated at time of serving: adding the cream and sherry then.
22. Also may be frozen.

**APRICOT LOAF**

**Ingredients:**
- 2 cups sifted all-purpose flour
- 1 cup sugar
- 2 teaspoons baking powder
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1 egg unbeaten
- 1 cup milk
- 2 tablespoons melted shortening or salad oil
- 1 to 2 cups dried apricots, washed and drained, cut in fourths with scissors
- 1 tablespoon grated orange rind (optional)

**Steps:**
1. Sift dry ingredients together into small bowl.
2. Add egg, milk and shortening.
3. Mix until just blended, about 30 seconds.
4. Cut apricots in fourths with scissors and fold in with orange rind, being careful not to overmix the batter.
5. Pour into greased 9 x 5 x 3 inch loaf pan.
6. Bake in oven 350° for 55 to 60 minutes.
7. Remove from pan and cool before cutting.

**To Mix by Hand Steps:**
1. Sift dry ingredients into bowl.
2. Beat egg and add with milk and shortening to dry ingredients.
3. Mix until just blended.
4. Fold in apricots and orange rind, being careful not to overmix.
5. Bake as stated above.

- ½ cup water
- 1 jar (28 oz.) mincemeat
- 3 egg whites
- ½ teaspoon cream of tartar
- 1 cup sugar
- 9 inch baked pie shell, cooled

**Steps:**

3. Wrap in wax paper, and chill until stiff (several hours or overnight).
4. With thin, sharp knife, cut slices ⅛ to 1/16 inches thick.
5. Place slices a little apart on ungreased baking sheet.
6. Bake at 400° for 8 to 10 minutes until lightly browned.

**Ingredients:**

- ¼ teaspoon salt
- ½ teaspoon nutmeg
- ½ teaspoon cinnamon
- ½ teaspoon allspice
- ¾ teaspoon cloves
- 2 cans cranberries, jellied
- 3 cups water

- Butter

**ALMOST INSTANT COFFEE CAKE**

**Ingredients:**
- ¾ cup firmly packed brown sugar
- 1 teaspoon cinnamon
- ½ cup (1 stick) butter, melted
- 2 packages 8 ounces each; refrigerated biscuit

**Steps:**
1. In a small bowl blend together sugar and cinnamon.
2. Put 2 tablespoons melted butter in bottom of mold.
3. Sprinkle in 4 tablespoons of the sugar-cinnamon mixture.
4. Dip each biscuit in melted butter.
5. Then dip in sugar-cinnamon mixture.
6. Place biscuits from one package in bottom of mold overlapping edges slightly.
7. Repeat a second layer, with remaining package of biscuits.
8. Bake at 400° for 12 to 15 minutes.
9. Allow to stand about 5 minutes.
10. Loosen around edge with spatula and turn out on serving plate.
11. Serve immediately.

**OATMEAL MUFFINS**

**Ingredients:**
- 1 cup quick cooking oatmeal
- 1 cup buttermilk
- 1 egg
- 1 cup sugar
- 1 cup cooking oil
- 1 cup sifted flour
- 1 teaspoon baking powder
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 2 teaspoon soda

**Steps:**
1. Soak oatmeal in buttermilk one hour.
2. Add egg and beat well.
3. Then add sugar and cooking oil.
4. Add flour sifted with baking powder, salt, and soda.
5. Stir just enough to mix well.
6. Fill well-greased muffin tins two thirds full and bake at 400° for 15 to 20 minutes.
7. These muffins keep fresh for several days.
8. They can be made several days ahead and warmed as needed.

**BROWNIE CAKE**

**Ingredients:**
- 1 cup butter
- ½ cup shortening
- 3 cups sugar
- 5 eggs
- 3 cups sifted flour
- ½ cup cocoa
- 1 cup milk
- 1 cup chopped pecans

**Steps:**
1. Cream butter, and Crisco until fluffy.
2. Add sugar gradually and beat until thoroughly mixed.
3. Add eggs one at a time and beat until smooth.
4. Add flour and cocoa alternately with milk, beginning and ending with flour.
5. Pour into greased tube pan.
6. Sprinkle chopped pecans on top.
7. Put into cold oven.
8. Bake at 300° for 2 hours.
