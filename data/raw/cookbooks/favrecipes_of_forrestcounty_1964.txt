**ARROZ CON POLLO (CHICKEN WITH RICE)**

**Ingredients:**
- 1 stewing chicken, cut into pieces (save 4 cups broth)
- 1½ pounds small shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 2 cans mushrooms, stems and pieces
- 1 box frozen English peas
- 1 can (4 oz) pimento, sliced
- 2 small onions, chopped fine
- ½ green pepper, chopped fine
- 2 cups canned tomatoes
- 2 teaspoons salt
- 2 teaspoons paprika
- 4 tablespoons fat
- 2 cups raw rice
- ½ cup parsley, chopped

**Steps:**
1. Boil chicken and save 4 cups of broth.
2. Brown rice in hot fat.
3. Add chicken broth, bring to a boil, and simmer.
4. When rice is half done, add frozen peas, cubed chicken pieces, whole shrimp, mushrooms, pimento, onion, green pepper, salt, paprika, and tomatoes.
5. Simmer until peas, shrimp, and rice are done, stirring occasionally with a fork.
6. Ensure all broth is absorbed.
7. Garnish with parsley.
8. Serve with guacamole in a lettuce cup, garnished with tomato wedges in a Mexican atmosphere.

**BEEF CASSEROLE**

**Ingredients:**
- 2 cups leftover pot roast or 1 pound ground beef
- 1 large onion, coarsely cut
- 3 stalks celery, coarsely cut
- 1 green pepper, coarsely cut
- 2 tablespoons butter
- 1 can (10½ oz) mushroom soup
- ½ cup water
- ½ teaspoon salt
- Dash of black pepper
- 1 can (3 oz) chow mein noodles

**Steps:**
1. Preheat oven to 325°F.
2. If using ground beef, cook until browned in 1 tablespoon butter; if using pot roast, chop into small cubes and cook for several minutes in 1 tablespoon butter.
3. Transfer beef to a medium-sized casserole dish.
4. In the same pan, add remaining butter and cook chopped onion, celery, and green pepper until limp.
5. Stir in mushroom soup, water, salt, and pepper, then pour over beef in the casserole dish and mix well.
6. Cover and bake for 30 minutes.
7. Remove cover, sprinkle chow mein noodles on top, and bake for an additional 10 minutes.

**CHICKEN LIVERS ON TOAST POINTS**

**Ingredients:**
- 2 pounds chicken livers
- 1¾ cups flour
- 1/8 teaspoon black pepper
- 8 slices salt pork or country bacon
- 2 medium onions, finely chopped
- ½ pound thin-sliced mushrooms
- 2 tablespoons butter
- 5 cups chicken broth
- 2 or 3 drops Kitchen Bouquet (optional)
- Pinch of nutmeg
- 1½ cups sherry
- 1 cup sour cream
- Toast points
- Paprika, for garnish

**Steps:**
1. Cut chicken livers in half and toss in 1 cup flour mixed with 1/8 teaspoon pepper.
2. Sauté salt pork or bacon until very crisp, remove, drain on paper towels, and keep warm.
3. Sauté livers in the drippings until cooked, then remove and drain on paper towels.
4. Sauté onions in the same pan until almost tender, add mushrooms, and cook until tender; remove and add to livers.
5. Add 2 tablespoons butter to the drippings, stir in ¾ cup flour, and cook, stirring constantly, until flour begins to brown (avoid burning).
6. Add chicken broth and stir until smooth and thickened.
7. If the sauce looks pale, add Kitchen Bouquet for color.
8. Season with nutmeg, salt, and pepper to taste.
9. Return livers, onions, and mushrooms to the sauce and heat through.
10. Stir in sour cream and sherry.
11. Serve on toast points, sprinkle with crumbled pork or bacon, and dust with paprika.
12. Note: Can be prepared ahead, reheated at serving time (add cream and sherry then), or frozen.

**CRANBERRY CHICKEN LAYER**

**Ingredients:**
- **Layer I:**
  - 1 envelope unflavored gelatin
  - ¼ cup cold water
  - 1 pound can whole cranberry sauce
  - 1 (9 oz) can crushed pineapple
  - ½ cup chopped pecans or other nuts
  - 1 tablespoon lemon juice
- **Layer II:**
  - 1 envelope unflavored gelatin
  - ¼ cup chicken stock
  - 1 cup mayonnaise
  - ½ cup chicken stock
  - ½ cup diced celery
  - 2 tablespoons chopped parsley
- Salad greens, mayonnaise, and pecan halves for garnish

**Steps:**
1. **Layer I:** Soften 1 envelope gelatin in ¼ cup cold water, dissolve over hot water.
2. Add cranberry sauce, crushed pineapple, chopped pecans, and lemon juice to gelatin.
3. Pour into a 10 x 16 x 1½-inch baking dish rubbed with salad oil, chill until set.
4. **Layer II:** Soften 1 envelope gelatin in ¼ cup chicken stock, dissolve over hot water.
5. Blend mayonnaise, ½ cup chicken stock, diced celery, and chopped parsley with gelatin.
6. Pour over the set first layer and chill until firm.
7. Cut into squares, invert onto salad greens, and top with mayonnaise and a pecan half.

**CAULIFLOWER WITH ONION RINGS**

**Ingredients:**
- 2 medium cauliflowers (or 2 packages frozen)
- 1½ cups water
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1 small can evaporated milk
- 4 tablespoons butter
- 4 tablespoons flour
- 1/8 teaspoon pepper
- 1 cup grated yellow cheese
- 1 can fried onion rings

**Steps:**
1. Divide cauliflower into florets and cook until just tender; drain and save stock.
2. Add enough stock to evaporated milk to make 2 cups.
3. Make a sauce with 4 tablespoons butter, 4 tablespoons flour, 1/8 teaspoon pepper, and the 2 cups stock-milk mixture.
4. In a 1½- or 2-quart casserole, layer half the cauliflower, half the cheese, half the sauce, and half the onion rings; repeat layers.
5. Bake at 350°F for 25–30 minutes until very hot and bubbly.
6. Note: Can be made several hours or a day ahead and refrigerated until 30 minutes before serving.

**HUNGRY BOY CASSEROLE**

**Ingredients:**
- **Casserole:**
  - 1 pound ground meat
  - 1 cup diced celery
  - 1 cup chopped onion
  - ½ cup green peppers
  - 1 clove minced garlic
  - 1¼ cups water
  - ¾ cup tomato paste (6 oz can)
  - ¾ teaspoon salt
  - 1 teaspoon paprika
  - ½ teaspoon Accent
  - 1 pound can pork and beans
  - 1 can lima beans, drained
- **Biscuits:**
  - 1½ cups flour
  - 2 teaspoons baking powder
  - ½ teaspoon salt
  - ¼ cup shortening
  - 1 egg
  - Milk (enough to make ½ cup with egg)
  - 1 tablespoon salad oil
  - ½ cup sliced stuffed olives
  - ½ cup slivered almonds
  - ½ teaspoon paprika
  - 1 cup reserved meat mixture

**Steps:**
1. **Casserole:** Sauté ground meat, celery, onion, green peppers, and garlic in a large skillet; drain.
2. Add water, tomato paste, salt, paprika, and Accent; simmer for 5 minutes.
3. Reserve 1 cup of meat mixture for biscuits.
4. Add pork and beans and lima beans to the remaining meat mixture, then transfer to an 8 x 13-inch or 2-quart baking dish.
5. **Biscuits:** Sift flour, baking powder, and salt; cut in shortening.
6. Combine egg with enough milk to make ½ cup, add to flour mixture, and stir until dough clings together.
7. Knead dough lightly about 12 times, then roll into a triangle.
8. Brush with salad oil, combine reserved meat, olives, almonds, and paprika, and spread on dough.
9. Roll dough as for a jelly roll, slice into 1-inch biscuits, and place on top of casserole.
10. Dot biscuits with butter and sprinkle with paprika.
11. Bake at 425°F for 25–30 minutes.

**APRICOT SALAD WITH TOPPING**

**Ingredients:**
- **Salad:**
  - 2 packages orange jello
  - 3½ cups hot water
  - 16 marshmallows, cut fine
  - Liquid from apricots
  - 1 large can peeled apricots, cut and drained
  - 1 (7 oz) can crushed pineapple, drained
  - Liquid from pineapple
- **Topping:**
  - ½ cup sugar
  - 1 heaping tablespoon cornstarch
  - Dash of salt
  - ½ cup apricot juice
  - ½ cup pineapple juice
  - 1 teaspoon butter
  - ½ pint whipped cream

**Steps:**
1. **Salad:** Dissolve jello in hot water, cool, and add marshmallows, apricots, pineapple, and their liquids.

**COLE SLAW SOUFFLÉ SALAD**

- ½ cup cold water
- ¼ cup mayonnaise
- 2 tablespoons vinegar
- ½ teaspoon salt
- Dash of pepper
- 2 cups finely chopped cabbage
- 2 tablespoons minced green pepper
- 2 tablespoons minced onion
- ¼ teaspoon celery seeds

**Steps:**
1. Dissolve gelatin in hot water, add cold water, mayonnaise, vinegar, salt, and pepper; beat with an egg beater until well blended.

**APRICOT LOAF**

**Ingredients:**
- 2 cups sifted all-purpose flour
- 1 cup sugar
- 2 teaspoons baking powder
- ½ teaspoon salt
- 1 egg, unbeaten
- 1 cup milk
- 2 tablespoons melted shortening or salad oil
- 1 to 2 cups dried apricots, washed and drained
- 1 tablespoon grated orange rind (optional)

**Steps:**
1. Sift flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt into a small bowl.
2. Add egg, milk, and shortening; mix until just blended, about 30 seconds.
3. Cut apricots into fourths with scissors and fold into the batter with orange rind, being careful not to overmix.
4. Pour into a greased 9 x 5 x 3-inch loaf pan.
5. Bake at 350°F for 55–60 minutes.
6. Remove from pan and cool before cutting.
7. **By Hand:** Beat egg, then mix with milk and shortening; add to sifted dry ingredients, blend, fold in apricots and orange rind, and bake as above.

**ALMOST INSTANT COFFEE CAKE**

**Ingredients:**
- ¾ cup firmly packed brown sugar
- 1 teaspoon cinnamon
- ½ cup (1 stick) butter, melted
- 2 (8 oz) packages refrigerated biscuits

**Steps:**
1. Blend brown sugar and cinnamon in a small bowl.

**1-2-3-4 CAKE**

**Ingredients:**
- ½ pound soft butter (not melted)
- 2 cups sugar
- 4 large eggs
- 3¾ cups plain flour
- 1 cup milk
- 2 teaspoons baking powder
- 1 teaspoon vanilla

**Steps:**
1. Ensure all ingredients are at room temperature (out of the refrigerator for at least 1 hour).
2. Cream butter and sugar well.
3. Add eggs one at a time, beating after each addition.
4. Add milk and flour alternately, starting and ending with flour.
5. Sift baking powder with the last cup of flour.
6. Add vanilla to the last portion of milk.
7. Pour into a greased and floured tube pan.

**MINCEMEAT CHIFFON PIE**

- 1 envelope (1 tablespoon) unflavored gelatin
- ½ cup water
- 1 (28 oz) jar mincemeat
- 3 egg whites
- ¼ teaspoon cream of tartar
- ½ cup sugar
- 9-inch baked pie shell

**Steps:**
1. Soften gelatin in water in a saucepan, then heat over low heat, stirring until dissolved.
2. Remove from heat and stir in mincemeat; chill until the mixture mounds slightly when dropped from a spoon.
3. Beat egg whites with cream of tartar until frothy, then gradually beat in sugar until stiff and glossy.

5. Pile into the baked pie shell and chill for at least 1 hour before serving.
