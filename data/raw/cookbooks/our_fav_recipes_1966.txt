

**GINGER ALE SALAD**

**Ingredients:**
- 1 tbsp. gelatin
- 2 tbsp. cold water
- 1/2 c. boiling water
- 1/2 c. ginger ale
- 2 tbsp. lemon juice
- 1 c. sliced peaches or pears
- 1 c. orange sections
- 1/4 c. fresh strawberries, sliced
- 1/4 c. grapes, halved or
- 1/2 c. diced pineapple

**Steps:**
1. Soften gelatin in cold water.
2. Add boiling water, ginger ale, lemon juice and sugar.
3. Cool, then add fruits.
4. Pour into a 1 quart mold or 8 1/2 cup individual molds and chill 6 to 8 hours or overnight.
5. Makes 8 servings.

**TWENTY FOUR HOUR SALAD**

**Ingredients:**
- 2 c. cherries, halved, pitted and drained
- 2 c. diced pineapple, drained
- 2 c. white seedless grapes
- 2 c. tiny marshmallows
- 1/4 c. slivered almonds, toasted lightly
- 2 eggs
- juice of 1 lemon
- 1/4 c. light cream
- 1 c. heavy cream
- 2 tbsp. sugar

**Steps:**
1. Beat eggs until light.
2. Add sugar, light cream, and lemon juice.
3. Cook in double boiler until smooth and thick, stirring constantly.
4. Let cool.
5. Whip heavy cream and fold into above mixture.
6. Mix lightly with fruit, nuts and marshmallows.
7. Chill 24 hours.
8. Do not freeze.
9. Serve in lettuce cups.

**SEAFOAM SALAD**

**Ingredients:**
- 1 pkg. lime Jello
- 1 lg. cream cheese
- 1 med. can pears
- 1 carton whipping cream

**Steps:**
1. Drain pears and cut into bite size.
2. Heat 1 cup of pear juice and dissolve the Jello.
3. Have cream cheese at room temperature and add to pear juice and Jello while it is warm.
4. Refrigerate and start to congeal.
5. Add 1 cup of whipped cream and pears.
6. Congeal.
7. Cut into squares and serve on lettuce topped with mayonnaise and cherry.

**BEEF STEW**

**Ingredients:**
- 1 to 1 1/2 lbs. boneless beef, cut in 1 1/2" pieces
- 2 tbsp. flour
- 4 tbsp. shortening
- 2 c. boiling water
- minced garlic, opt.
- 4 med. potatoes
- 1 bay leaf
- 2 tsp. salt
- 4 carrots
- 1 c. chopped onion

**Steps:**
1. Roll meat in flour and brown with onion slowly for 15 minutes.
2. Add the bay leaf, half the water and half the salt.
3. Cover and simmer for 1 1/2 hours.
4. Add vegetables, remaining water and salt and garlic.
5. Cover again and cook 30 minutes more.
6. Thicken gravy with 3 tablespoons browned flour.
7. Serves 4.
8. Never boil a stew. Let it simmer gently.

**ICE BOX CAKE**

**Ingredients:**
- 3 pts. fresh strawberries (save out 12 nice ones for garnish, slice the rest)
- 1 egg plus 1 egg yolk, beaten
- 1 1/2 tsp. vanilla (save 1/2 for whipped cream)
- 1 1/2 c. heavy cream, whipped
- 3/4 c. soft butter
- 2 c. vanilla wafer crumbs (reserve 2 tsp. for top)
- 3 c. powdered sugar, save 2 tbsp. for cream when whipped

**Steps:**
1. Cream butter and sugar, add beaten egg and 1 teaspoon vanilla.
2. Consistency should be pourable, but not watery. If mixture seems too thick, set the bowl in a pan of hot water and stir until thin enough to pour.
3. Put half the wafer crumbs on bottom of pan, a square pan of about 2 quart capacity is about right.
4. Pour half the cream mixture over the crumb layer, add half the berries, then half of whipped cream (repeat layers).
5. Sprinkle reserved crumbs on top.
6. This dessert should be served partially frozen. If you put it in the freezer for 1 hour, it will be perfect.

**REFRIGERATOR ROLLS**

**Ingredients:**
- 1 3/4 c. water, warm
- 1/2 c. gran. sugar
- 1 egg, unbeaten
- 6 c. sifted all purpose flour
- 2 pkgs. active dry yeast
- 1 tbsp. sugar
- 1/4 c. soft butter or marg.
- 1 tbsp. butter, melted

**Steps:**
1. Pour warm water into a large bowl.
2. Sprinkle the yeast over the water; add sugar and salt; stir to dissolve completely.
3. Add eggs, soft butter and 3 cups flour.
4. With wooden spoon or electric mixer at medium speed, beat very hard 2 minutes or until smooth.
5. Gradually add 1 cup flour, beating hard after each addition.
6. Using hands, work remaining 2 cups flour into dough, with hands, until smooth and elastic.
7. Brush tip of the dough with melted butter; cover with a double thickness of saran or with a damp towel.
8. Let the dough rise in refrigerator at least 2 hours, or until it is double in bulk.
9. Punch down dough, and refrigerate.
10. Store in refrigerator 1 to 3 days punching it down once a day.
11. Shape and bake at 400 to 425 degrees for 15 to 20 minutes.

- 2 tbsp. Karo syrup
- 1/2 tsp. salt
- 1 3-oz. pkg. strawberry Jello
- 1 to 1 1/2 pt. fresh strawberries

**Steps:**
1. Mix and boil above ingredients, except Jello and strawberries, until thick and completely clear, about 4 minutes.
2. Remove and add Jello, stir until dissolved.
3. Cook pie pastry and while hot, sprinkle 3 tablespoons of sugar over shell.
4. Let cool.
5. Wash and drain berries, place in pastry whole.
6. Pour syrup mixture over berries.
7. Let cool.
8. Whipped cream is used as topping and may be put on pie when served in individual pieces or on whole pie before cut.

**CHOCOLATE PIE**

**Ingredients:**
- 2 sqs. (2 oz.) unsweetened chocolate
- 2 tbsp. butter
- 1/3 c. flour
- 1 c. sugar
- 2 1/2 c. milk, scalded
- 3 eggs, separated
- 3/4 tsp. vanilla
- 1/4 tsp. salt

**Steps:**
1. Melt chocolate and butter over hot water in top of double boiler.
2. Mix flour, sugar and salt and stir into chocolate mixing until well blended.
3. Add 1 cup of the hot milk and stir until smooth.
4. Add remaining milk and continue to cook, stirring frequently until mixture is smooth and thickened, about 15 minutes.
5. Beat egg yolks well; stir in a little of the chocolate mixture, then pour back into rest of hot mixture and cook 2 minutes, stirring constantly.
6. Remove from heat, stir in vanilla.
7. Pour immediately into cooled pie shell.
8. Top with meringue or whipped cream.

**FRIED PIES**

**Ingredients:**
- 3 c. flour, plain
- 1/3 c. buttermilk
- 1/4 tsp. soda
- 2 tbsp. sugar
- 1/2 c. shortening
- 1/2 tsp. salt
- cooked fruit as filling

**Steps:**
1. Sift flour into a large mixing bowl.
2. Press down the center and add buttermilk, soda, sugar, shortening, and salt.
3. Work like biscuit with your hands.
4. Work till dough is real stiff.
5. Roll pretty thin and use any kind of cooked fruit as filling.
6. Use saucer to rounds of dough, place fruit on 1/2, fold other half over and press edges together.
7. Fry in hot shortening or bake on cookie sheet 350 degrees till brown.

**FROZEN LEMON PIE**

**Ingredients:**
- 1 tall can evap. milk
- 1 c. gran. sugar
- 2-3 lemons, juice only
- graham crackers

**Steps:**
1. Put milk into ice tray and let chill until a crust about 1/4" forms all over the pan.

4. Add sugar gradually, then lemon juice.
5. Pour into tray and cover with graham cracker crumbs.

**SPECIAL K COOKIES**

**Ingredients:**
- 2 c. sifted all purpose flour
- 1/2 tsp. baking soda
- 1 c. soft butter or marg.
- 1 c. powdered sugar, sifted
- 2 eggs
- 1/2 c. flaked coconut
- 4 c. Kellogg's Special K cereal
- 1 tsp. baking powder
- 1/2 tsp. salt
- 1 c. brown sugar, firmly packed
- 1 tsp. almond flavoring
- 1/2 c. finely chopped pecans

**Steps:**
1. Sift together flour, baking powder, soda and salt.
2. Beat butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
3. Add eggs and vanilla; beat well.
4. Add sifted dry ingredients; mix well.
5. Add coconut and pecans, stirring in well.
6. Stir in Special K a cup at the time.
7. Drop by tablespoonsfull onto an ungreased baking sheet.
8. Bake in a moderate oven; 300 degrees 10 minutes, or until lightly browned.

- 1/8 tsp. salt
- 1 cup cold water
- 1 1/2 ounces white Karo
- 1 1/2 ounces strawberry gelatin
- 1/8 ounce red coloring if desired
- 1 pint of fresh strawberries

**Steps:**

**PUNCH**

**Ingredients:**
- 3 tbsp. tea leaves
- 4 cups boiling water
- 1 1/2 or 2 cups sugar
- 1 1/2 cups boiling water
- 8 whole cloves
- 6 oranges or 1 can concentrate
- 6 lemons or 1 can concentrate
- 1 46-oz. can pineapple juice
- 1 tsp. almond extract
- 3 qts. ice water or gingerale
- 1 bottle Maraschino cherry juice

**Steps:**
1. Steep tea in 4 cups boiling water 5 minutes.
2. Strain and cool.
3. Make a syrup by boiling 1 1/2 cups water, 1 1/2 cups sugar, cloves, 1 orange, 1 lemon peel for 5 minutes and cool.
4. When cool combine tea, syrup and other ingredients except chilled water or gingerale.
5. Allow to stand 1 hour or until ready to serve.
6. Add chilled water and chipped ice.
7. Serves 25.

**HOT SPICED TEA**

**Ingredients:**
- 4 qts. water
- 1 tsp. whole cloves
- 1 stick cinnamon
- 1/3 cup tea or 15 tea bags
- 1 1/4 cups sugar
- 1 cup orange juice
- 3/4 cup fresh lemon juice

**Steps:**
1. Add spices to water.
2. Bring water to full boil.
3. Remove from heat.
4. Add tea immediately.
5. Brew 4 minutes.
6. Strain.
7. Add sugar, stir until dissolved.
8. Add fruit juices.
9. To reheat for serving place over low heat.
