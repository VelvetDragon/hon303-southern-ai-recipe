- 2 tbsp. cold water
- 1/2 c. boiling water

- 2 tbsp. lemon juice
- 1 c. sliced peaches or pears

**TWENTY FOUR HOUR SALAD**

**Ingredients:**
- 2 c. cherries, halved, pitted, and drained
- 2 c. diced pineapple, drained
- 2 c. white seedless grapes
- 2 c. tiny marshmallows
- 1/4 c. slivered almonds, toasted lightly
- 2 eggs
- Juice of 1 lemon
- 1/4 c. light cream
- 1 c. heavy cream
- 2 tbsp. sugar

**Steps:**
1. Beat eggs until light.
2. Add sugar, light cream, and lemon juice.
3. Cook in a double boiler until smooth and thick, stirring constantly.
4. Let cool.
5. Whip heavy cream and fold into the above mixture.
6. Mix lightly with fruit, nuts, and marshmallows.
7. Chill 24 hours. Do not freeze.
8. Serve in lettuce cups.

**BEEF STEW**

**Ingredients:**
- 1 to 1 1/2 lbs. boneless beef, cut in 1 1/2" pieces
- 2 tbsp. flour
- 4 tbsp. shortening
- 2 c. boiling water
- Minced garlic (optional)
- 4 medium potatoes
- 1 bay leaf
- 2 tsp. salt
- 4 carrots
- 1 c. chopped onion

**Steps:**
1. Roll meat in flour and brown with onion slowly for 15 minutes.

**ICE BOX CAKE**

**Ingredients:**
- 3 pts. fresh strawberries (save out 12 nice ones for garnish, slice the rest)
- 1 egg plus 1 egg yolk, beaten

**REFRIGERATOR ROLLS**

**Ingredients:**
- 1 3/4 c. water, warm
- 1/2 c. granulated sugar
- 1 egg, unbeaten
- 6 c. sifted all-purpose flour
- 2 pkgs. active dry yeast
- 1 tbsp. sugar
- 1/4 c. soft butter or margarine
- 1 tbsp. butter, melted

**Steps:**
1. Pour warm water into a large bowl.
2. Sprinkle the yeast over the water; add sugar and salt; stir to dissolve completely.
3. Add eggs, soft butter, and 3 cups flour.
4. With a wooden spoon or electric mixer at medium speed, beat very hard for 2 minutes or until smooth.
5. Gradually add 1 cup flour, beating hard after each addition.
6. Using hands, work remaining 2 cups flour into dough, with hands, until smooth and elastic.
7. Brush the top of the dough with melted butter; cover with a double thickness of saran or with a damp towel.
8. Let the dough rise in the refrigerator at least 2 hours, or until it is double in bulk.
9. Punch down dough and refrigerate.
10. Store in refrigerator 1 to 3 days, punching it down once a day.
11. Shape and bake at 400 to 425 degrees for 15 to 20 minutes.

**STRAWBERRY PIE**

**Ingredients:**
- 1 c. water or 17-Up + 2 oz.
- 1 c. sugar
- 2 tbsp. cornstarch
- 2 tbsp. Karo syrup
- 1/2 tsp. salt
- 1 3-oz. pkg. strawberry Jello
- 1 to 1 1/2 pt. fresh strawberries

**Steps:**
1. Mix and boil above ingredients, except Jello and strawberries, until thick and completely clear, about 4 minutes.
2. Remove and add Jello, stir until dissolved.

4. Wash and drain berries, place in pastry whole.
5. Pour syrup mixture over berries.
6. Let cool.
7. Whipped cream is used as topping and may be put on pie when served in individual pieces or on whole pie before cut.

**PUNCH**

**Ingredients:**
- 3 tbsp. tea leaves
- 4 c. boiling water
- 1 1/2 or 2 c. sugar
- 1 1/2 c. boiling water
- 8 whole cloves
- 6 oranges or 1 can concentrate
- 6 lemons or 1 can concentrate
- 1 46-oz. can pineapple juice
- 1 tsp. almond extract
- 3 qts. ice water or ginger ale
- 1 bottle maraschino cherry juice

**Steps:**
1. Steep tea in 4 cups boiling water for 5 minutes. Strain and cool.
2. Make a syrup by boiling 1 1/2 cups water, 1 1/2 cups sugar, cloves, 1 orange, and 1 lemon peel for 5 minutes and cool.
3. When cool, combine tea, syrup, and other ingredients except chilled water or ginger ale.
4. Allow to stand 1 hour or until ready to serve.
5. Add chilled water and chipped ice.
6. Serves 25.

**HOT SPICED TEA**

**Ingredients:**
- 4 qts. water
- 1 tsp. whole cloves
- 1 stick cinnamon
- 1/3 c. tea or 15 tea bags
- 1 1/4 c. sugar
- 1 c. orange juice
- 3/4 c. fresh lemon juice

**Steps:**
1. Add spices to water. Bring water to a full boil.
2. Remove from heat. Add tea immediately.
3. Brew 4 minutes. Strain.
4. Add sugar, stir until dissolved.
