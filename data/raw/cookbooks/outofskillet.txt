**BEATEN BISCUIT**

**Ingredients:**
- 4 cups flour
- 1 tbsp lard
- 1 tsp salt
- Cold water, as needed

**Steps:**
1. Mix flour, lard, and salt, adding enough cold water to make a stiff dough.
2. Place on a marble slab and beat with a bat or rolling pin until dough begins to blister, at least 500 strokes.
3. Roll out about 0.5 inch thick, cut with a small biscuit cutter, and prick the top of each biscuit with a fork.
4. Bake in a slow oven until golden brown.

**SPOON BREAD**

**Ingredients:**
- 1.5 cups water
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 cup white corn meal
- Large piece of butter
- 2 egg yolks
- 1 cup sweet milk
- 2 egg whites, well beaten

**Steps:**
1. Bring water and salt to a boil, then gradually stir in corn meal.

3. Mix well, then add well-beaten egg whites.
4. Pour into a greased baking dish and bake in a moderate oven for about 30 minutes.

- 1 No. 2 can tomato juice
- 1 cup sugar
- 0.5 cup vinegar
- 1 large piece celery, chopped
- 1 medium onion, chopped
- Small piece garlic
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 1 tbsp pickling spice
- 1.5 envelopes gelatine
- Cold water, for soaking
- Chopped olives, optional
- Pecans, optional
- Celery, optional
- Shrimp, optional

**Steps:**
1. Boil tomato juice, sugar, vinegar, celery, onion, garlic, salt, pepper, and pickling spice for 10 minutes.
2. If too thick, add a little water.
3. Strain and, while hot, add gelatine soaked in cold water for 5 minutes.
4. Serve plain after chilling, or add chopped olives, pecans, celery, or shrimp as it begins to congeal.

**TIPSY CAKE**

**Ingredients:**
- 3 eggs
- 1.125 cups flour
- 1 cup sugar
- 3 tbsp cold water
- 1 tsp baking powder
- 0.33 tsp salt
- Grated rind of 0.5 lemon
- 4 cups milk
- 4 eggs
- 8 tbsp sugar
- 0.5 tsp salt
- 1 tsp vanilla
- 1.5 cups blanched almonds
- 12 tbsp whiskey or sherry
- Whipped cream, for topping

**Steps:**
1. Separate 3 eggs, beat yolks with sugar, add water, lemon rind, and flour sifted twice with baking powder and 0.33 tsp salt.
2. Fold in beaten egg whites, pour into a greased 9-inch square pan, and bake at 320°F until done.
3. Heat 4 cups milk, combine 4 eggs, 8 tbsp sugar, and 0.5 tsp salt, and stir into hot milk.
4. Cook over hot water until custard coats a spoon, add vanilla, and chill.
5. Split cake into two layers, stick almonds into each cut side, using half the nuts per layer.
6. Sprinkle one layer with half the whiskey, pour over half the chilled custard.
7. Place the second layer almond-side up, sprinkle with remaining whiskey, and pour over remaining custard.
8. Top with a thick layer of whipped cream.

- Food coloring, optional

**Steps:**
1. Cook 1 cup sugar and 0.5 cup water until it spins a thread, then pour over stiffly beaten egg whites and beat well.
2. Cook 2.5 cups sugar, 1 cup water, and corn syrup until very brittle in cold water.
3. Pour this syrup over the egg white mixture.
4. Add a drop or two of oil of peppermint and optional food coloring, beating until it holds its shape.
5. Drop by spoonfuls onto waxed paper.

**CHOCOLATE FUDGE**

**Ingredients:**
- 3 cups sugar
- 7 tbsp cocoa
- 3 tbsp white corn syrup
- 1.5 cups sweet milk
- 1 tsp vanilla
- Butter, size of an egg
- 1 cup nuts

**Steps:**
1. Stir sugar, cocoa, corn syrup, and milk well, then cook until a small amount hardens in a saucer.

**EGGNOG**

**Ingredients:**
- 12 eggs, separated
- 1.5 pints whiskey
- 2 pints cream
- 1 pint sugar
- Nutmeg, for sprinkling

**Steps:**
1. Beat egg yolks well, then gradually add sugar, continuing to beat.
2. Add whiskey a little at a time.
3. Add 1 pint unbeaten cream, then stiffly beaten egg whites, stirring well.
4. Whip remaining 1 pint cream and stir in.
5. Chill overnight, then shake nutmeg on top of each cup before serving.

**WATERMELON PICKLE**

**Ingredients:**
- 3 lbs watermelon rind
- Alum water, for soaking
- Clear water, for soaking
- Weak ginger water, for boiling
- 3 cups sugar
- 1 pint vinegar
- 1 tbsp cloves
- 1 tbsp whole spice

**Steps:**
1. Prepare rind and soak in alum water for 24 hours.

**SPICED GRAPES OR MUSCADINES**

**Ingredients:**
- 5 lbs grapes or muscadines
- 0.75 cup water
- 2.5 lbs sugar
- 2 tbsp cinnamon
- 2 tbsp allspice
- 0.5 tbsp ground cloves
- 0.5 cup vinegar, to taste

**Steps:**
1. Wash and pulp grapes.
2. Boil skins with water until tender.
3. Cook pulps in their own juice until seeds can be removed through a colander.
4. Mix skins and pulps, then add sugar, cinnamon, allspice, cloves, and vinegar.
5. Boil, stirring occasionally, until rather thick.
6. Seal in jars.
