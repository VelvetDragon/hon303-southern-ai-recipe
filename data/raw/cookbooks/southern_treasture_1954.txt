**CUCUMBER PICKLES**

**Ingredients:**
- 1 gal. cucumbers
- 1 c. coarse, sack salt
- 1 gal. water
- 1 Tbsp. alum
- Vinegar (enough to cover)
- 5 lbs. cucumbers (weighed after cutting)
- 3 lbs. sugar
- Pickling spices (in 3 small bags)
- 2 Tbsp. Wesson oil

**Steps:**
1. Soak cucumbers in brine of salt and water for 1 week or longer.
2. Remove from brine, cut into 1/8 or 1/4 inch round pieces.
3. Place in alum water (1 Tbsp. alum to 1 gal. water), let stand overnight.
4. Remove from alum water, cover with vinegar for 24 hours.
5. Drain vinegar, weigh 5 lbs. cucumbers to 3 lbs. sugar.
6. Layer cucumbers and sugar in a large jar, placing a bag of pickling spices at the bottom, middle, and top.
7. When sugar dissolves, add Wesson oil to the top.
8. Preserve for one year.

**ICE CREAM PICKLES**

**Ingredients:**
- 2 gal. cucumbers
- 2 c. salt
- 2 Tbsp. alum
- Boiling water (enough to cover)
- Vinegar (for syrup)
- Sugar (for syrup)
- Spices (for syrup)

**Steps:**
1. Pour boiling water over salt and cucumbers, let stand overnight.
2. Drain, cover with clear boiling water, let stand overnight, drain.
3. Make syrup of vinegar, sugar, and spices, bring to boil, pour over pickles.
4. Let stand for 3 days.
5. On the fourth day, reheat syrup, pack pickles in jars, pour hot syrup over pickles, and seal.

**GREEN TOMATO PICKLES**

**Ingredients:**
- 7 lb. full-grown green tomatoes
- 2 gal. water
- 3 c. powdered lime
- 5 lb. sugar
- 3 pt. vinegar
- 2 heaping Tbsp. whole, mixed spice (in bag)

**Steps:**
1. Wash tomatoes, cut out stems, slice 1/4 inch thick.
2. Soak in lime and water in a large pot for 24 hours.
3. Soak in clear water for 4 hours, changing water every 30 minutes.
4. Drain well, place in large pot.
5. Mix sugar and vinegar in another pot, bring to a rolling boil, pour over tomatoes, let stand overnight.
6. Add spice bag, cook slowly for 1 hour.
7. Put in jars and seal.

**SWEET CRISP CUCUMBER PICKLES**

**Ingredients:**
- Medium size cucumbers
- 2 tsp. alum
- 1 qt. water (for alum solution)
- 1 qt. sugar
- 1 qt. vinegar
- Spices (to taste)
- Ice water (equal parts ice and water)

**Steps:**
1. Slice cucumbers 1/8 inch thick, soak overnight in alum water (2 tsp. alum to 1 qt. water).
2. Drain, cover with same alum solution, simmer 2 hours (do not boil).
3. Drain, place in ice water until cold.
4. Make syrup of sugar, vinegar, and spices, boil until thick.
5. Place cucumbers in syrup one jar at a time, bring to boil, replace in jar, let drain.
6. Repeat until all cucumbers are processed.
7. Boil syrup to thicken, pour over pickles, seal.

**BAKED BEANS**

**Ingredients:**
- 1 1/2 c. dried beans
- 1/4 c. chopped onion
- 1/4 lb. sliced salt pork
- 2 Tbsp. or more dark molasses
- 2 or 3 Tbsp. catsup
- 1 Tbsp. dry mustard
- 1 tsp. salt
- 1/2 c. boiling bean water
- Well-seasoned stock or reserved bean water (as needed)

**Steps:**
1. Soak beans covered overnight.

4. Place in greased baker, decorate with salt pork, bake covered at 250°F for 6-8 hours.
5. Add stock or reserved bean water if dry, uncover for last hour.

**CHICKEN PIE**

**Ingredients:**
- 1 c. buttermilk
- 1/2 tsp. salt
- 1/2 tsp. soda
- 1/8 c. shortening
- 2 c. flour (more or less as needed)
- 1/2 or 3 lb. chicken
- 4 Tbsp. butter
- 2 1/2 tsp. black pepper
- 1 qt. water (for boiling)

**Steps:**
1. Mix together buttermilk, soda, salt, and shortening.
2. Add enough flour to make a stiff dough. Work it until quite firm.
3. Roll out about 2/3 of dough very thin. Cut into small pieces for dumplings.
4. Cut the chicken in pieces and boil in about 1 qt. of water until tender. Salt to suit taste.
5. Place the chicken and broth in 10 inch pan or casserole.
6. Drop in dumplings. Sprinkle in pepper and dot with butter.
7. Roll out remainder of dough large as pan and place on top.
8. Bake until brown, brush with butter and serve hot.
